# Community Contribution Pathways

## Scientific Software

- `pyOpenSci` for Python once packaging metadata, locked CI, tests, docs,
  citation guidance, and governance artifacts are ready.
- `rOpenSci` only if an R wrapper or port is justified, and only after
  `testthat`, `roxygen2`, `pkgdown`, CRAN-style checks, and reverse-dependency
  awareness are in place.
- JOSS or similar publication venues when citation, license, authorship, and
  reproducibility artifacts are complete.

## Contribution Prerequisites

Before any external contribution claim, the project should have:

- a documented license,
- `CONTRIBUTING.md`,
- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`,
- `CITATION.cff` or equivalent citation guidance,
- validation evidence for the claimed behavior,
- a clear privacy/data-governance statement,
- and release notes or changelog discipline.

## Health and Public Sector

- Health economics and health informatics communities for domain feedback.
- Government digital-health and govtech communities for policy and
  interoperability discussion.
